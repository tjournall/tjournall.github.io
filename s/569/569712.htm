For the new statute to be content-neutral, it must be unrelated to free expression, and neutral with respect to content. The new statute provides: "Whoever knowingly mutilates, defaces, burns, maintains on the floor or ground, or tramples upon any flag of the United States shall be fined not more than $1,000 or imprisoned for not more than one year, or both." Unlike the Texas law invalidated by Johnson, this federal statute makes no reference to "desecration" and does not turn on whether the proscribed conduct "will seriously offend" others.
