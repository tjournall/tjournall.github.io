Modern music

37 –ø—Ä–æ—Å–º–æ—Ç—Ä–æ–≤

In the modern world, music is most often perceived as entertainment. To its sounds, people dance at discos, listen to it to pass the time in transport, turn it on to create a background while doing some household chores. They go into it when they don't want to think about anything, when they try to forget about some troubles and problems.

Classical works are perceived quite differently. There are no words needed, you can't make music videos for this music. In them, every sound touches some deep chord of the soul, awakens what we have long and persistently hidden from the outside eye. We can entrust our thoughts, grief, and joy to classical music. It is no accident that Fr. Balzac said: "Beethoven and Mozart were often the confidants of my heart's secrets." The works of these great composers live their own lives, and you can talk to them, argue with them, or agree with them as with a living being. And many writers and poets, artists and sculptors, it was classical music that inspired the creation of outstanding works.

In fact, according to the present, relaxation is brought by classical works that were created in order to touch all the strings of the human soul. In today's world, despite the increasing popularization of club tracks, classics still have a strong place. After all, the works of the classics are their call to eternity, to future generations, so that we, who are just this future generation, can correctly evaluate their thoughts and desires. That is why today a large number of people visit the opera and the Philharmonic, in order to merge with eternity, at least for a few hours.

Limitations of classical music

The word classical comes from the Latin classicus, which means exemplary; but in this case we are not talking only about individual examples to follow - classical music (not to be confused with the historical period "classicism") is a culture that includes several basic elements. For me, these elements are:

author-performer tradition (technological way of life);

acoustic tradition (engineering culture);

academic tradition (standards of teaching and research).

Musicologists can vie with each other to complete this list, telling about a special musical language, the most important artistic principles, schools and associations that have become "classical", defeating their less successful competitors in the course of history. Friends, go for a moment beyond the definitions invented by someone once and just observe the world around you. You will see that all the musicians of the planet Earth are the same by nature - they want to express themselves and earn their craft; but among them there are those who practice three types of activities at the same time: musical notation, maintaining the concert infrastructure of the philharmonic type, and honoring Tchaikovsky and Bach. I suggest calling these respected people and their values classical music, rather than archives and beautiful stories.It is necessary to understand that the classical musical tradition dominated not by chance, but because it turned out to be consistent from a scientific, philosophical and economic point of view. She easily explained any musical manifestations of a person, adapted it and continues to do so for many of our contemporaries, but does not perform this function for me personally.

I propose to consider modern music as those interrelated phenomena that are obviously evolving and gaining strength among musicians and listeners, but at the same time can no longer exist within the framework of classical traditions, they conflict with them. We will focus primarily on music recordings as an art and business.

In other researchers, you can see the terms "popular" or "new music", but for various reasons I prefer the word "modern". Yes, there is a concept of "modern academic music", so they say about the works of composers living in our days, but I suggest not to mix all this. Let the classics remain classics, no matter who does it.

1. Technological structure

Notes are a type of end product in the classical tradition. When we say the word "work", we mean notes - and vice versa. A musical notation is created by a composer, it has its own end user - a musician-performer.

Concert performance of works is the second type of "classical" final product, where the consumer is the listener. We can say that both the performer and the listener become co-authors of the composer during the concert, because the performer gives personality to the author's idea due to his sound wave, and the listener's reaction affects what is happening, gives it additional meaning, not to mention the fact that the listener invests money in production by buying a ticket. Economists will understand me well in this matter, and musicologists will say that although concert performance is a relatively independent form of artistic and creative activity, but the performer does not create a new work, his destiny is interpretation, interpretation. Let us listen to both, and use the word "work" only in relation to the musical notation, and "product" - in a broader sense.

So, these two types of products, musical notation (musical composition) and concert performance, make up the production structure of classical music. If you write sheet music for others or perform works yourself, you are a follower of the classical tradition of music production. It doesn't matter if you're a member of Radiohead or the London Symphony Orchestra, because what you do is convert notes into sound, following the instructions of the author.

For modern music, notes and performance are no longer enough, here both the final product and the artistic work becomes an audio recording or even a multimedia product, for example, a music video clip. Audio recordings are no longer created for performance, but for listening and playback (the phenomenon of DJ "performance" will be discussed in Chapter 2, section "Professional environment").

Agree, it is difficult to argue with the fact that music is primarily what we hear? Classical music allows for a situation where the author of the work is a deaf composer, but the process of creating an audio recording is impossible to imagine without the act of listening; deaf authors in this area can not be found, which is why the logic of modern music seems to me much more convincing. You can't imagine a deaf Norman Cook creating a piece of music from samples as part of the Fatboyslim project. Drawing historical parallels, let us recall the deaf Beethoven, who successfully continued his compositional activity in his time. I dare say that he would hardly be able to claim an important role in the creation of modern music, since he could not become a music producer. His work could be of some use to other producers, but nothing more.

The role of the classical composer in the studio process is no more important than the function of the screenwriter in film production. By the way, performing musicians can be compared to film actors, so we will conclude the analogy, which clearly shows that both the composer and the performer in modern music production are subordinate functions, even if they may in some cases become key. The main thing in this question is to understand and accept that without performers and composers, the creation of musical audio recordings is fundamentally possible, as well as the creation of films without screenwriters (documentaries) and actors (animated films).

Works of classical music are created for performance, modern music-for listening. The roles of the composer and performer have lost their dominant importance in the production of audio recordings, now they have a subordinate function.

Some audio recordings can be recorded with notes, but in order to write a full-fledged instruction for creating a work, you will have to sign the nature of the sound timbres, frequency characteristics and types of compression; come up with designations for special effects and different sound plans, and even such a recording without attached audio files (at least the original "raw" ones) is unlikely to have the necessary meaning, the necessary value.

Let's not forget about the works performed at concerts. You will say that they can also be recorded and then listened to as audio recordings. Indeed, we may come across an audio recording that will be called "Beethoven performed by such and such an orchestra", but for me the main difference between concert recordings is that they do not sound the best from sound reproducing devices. These records are something like an advertisement, a cultural and educational act, or a visual aid in the classical tradition. They can also play a supporting role to preserve the musical heritage (in case the musical notation is lost), but they themselves, in isolation from the musical material, are unlikely to be considered as new works of musical art. Concert recordings require not only listening, but also certain assumptions. About a " live "recording, we always know that it is "live", in fact, we immerse ourselves in the atmosphere and first of all imagine the event itself, evaluate the behavior of people who find themselves in the situation of a concert, but not the music and sound wave. The musicians are well aware of this, and try to record in the studio at every opportunity.

You can successfully argue with this idea, but as a small experiment - try to remember at least one world-famous music audio track recorded at a concert. My subscribers and I tried to do this, but we only offered Zemfira with " London Sky "and Opus with"Life is Life". It's a stretch to recall compositions that mimic the atmosphere of a live concert - in which you can hear shouts from the crowd, a discordant chorus of fans - but when checked, it turns out that they were all created in the studio.

Special attention should be paid to the works of composers who still get to the studios. In my opinion, these audio recordings are new works, works of modern music. Whether classical works (sheet music) are used in their creation, and what role they play, whether the composers ' copyrights will come into force - all this is not obvious and requires additional analysis. Depending on the timbres used, the editing techniques used, and the special effects applied, the musical material may be well guessed, or it may be distorted: so much so that if you try to "hear" the resulting audio recording with notes, you will get something completely different from the original musical text used. Just like in a movie, there may be nothing left of the original script in the end.

Using these examples, we can see that there is no free conversion of classical music to modern music and back at the technological level, which means that their products (works) differ significantly.

Is it a good thing that the author-performer tradition of music production is supported as an absolute and in many ways - an uncontested good in Russian society?

For a child who needs to develop fine motor skills, masterly possession of an instrument is a wonderful practice. The development of musical hearing, melodic and harmonic thinking is good, if, of course, it does not cause damage to other types of child development.

Many musicians are happy to spend their entire lives on tours, using their performing skills in orchestras, theater, show business; but the bias towards such routine work as performing music live, in my opinion, leads rather to degradation, simplification of modern musical creativity. Perhaps the classical composers were not prevented from making music every day in churches and at balls, but modern production processes are much more complex than musical notation. As an example, I will cite the "Beatles", who in the mid-60s abandoned tour activity, as a result of which their most famous and popular works appeared. And what would happen if many other music producers found the courage to reduce the number of performances (the economics of the question aside for now), and devoted the free time to creative growth?

The combination of classical and modern production traditions, of course, is possible, in addition-they mutually influence each other. Performing your music at concerts is an inspiring experience for aspiring musicians and a way to gather like-minded people for further work in the studio, and attending concerts is still an important form of cultural enrichment for a listener of any age; but ask yourself, which piece of music has made the greatest impact on your spiritual growth and life in general, heard at a concert or recorded in the studio? What should be more interesting for a creative person to leave behind-instructions for creating works, or the works themselves? For me, the answer is obvious, and someone still forces children to study the piano all day long and write musical dictation instead of spending at least half of this time analyzing the masterpieces of musical audio recordings and mastering the technologies of music production.

By the way, encouraging reports come from Moscow, where the City Department of Culture has planned to equip all music schools with recording studios and introduce a new subject in the program dedicated to the basics of creating modern music. Let's keep an eye on this experiment and hope for its success.

2. Engineering culture

It is also possible to trace the gap in the technological structures of the classical and modern traditions through engineering specialties related to music. Let's divide them into three types:

engineers serving the classical tradition; engineers who solve the problems of modern music; universal engineers. The only category of engineers who are obliged to be imbued with the ideas of the classical tradition is concert sound engineers. This profession was born out of a banal need for sound amplification. Engineers of this type should take into account all the shortcomings of the premises in which the concert will take place, and at the expense of the available technical means to ensure the solution of the tasks of performers and listeners. In some cases, these engineers can become co-authors, transforming the work beyond recognition, but most often this role is subordinate, because any concert can take place without their participation.

Engineering specialties that focus on the challenges of modern music are much more numerous, and the main ones are found in the studio process.

Studio sound engineers and engineers are a range of professions that includes recording, editing, mixing, and mastering engineers.

The multi-stage process involves a division of labor that is impossible without the function of a music producer, who is responsible for the final result. He is both the chief engineer and the artistic director.

In the classical tradition, engineers are poorly involved in music production; in the modern tradition, they become an integral part of it; at the same time, the function of the music producer appears as the chief sound engineer and artistic director.

Not always a single person acts as a music producer, it can be a group of people (co-producers), the main thing in this case is a competent combination of engineering, management and artistic solutions. The function of a producer is comparable to the role of a director in film production. Just as it is impossible to create a film without a director, so it is impossible to create an audio recording without a music producer, sooner or later someone must perform the most important function and take responsibility for the results of the production process.

Such a division of labor leads to the emergence of specialists in this field, which means that you can give historical examples, mention specific people. From an economic point of view, their work can be either hired or independent; from a legal point of view, they can be considered authors or not, this does not change their role in the production process. The path of most of them began with sound engineering, they were called sound producers (English: sound producer, and also epdineg, sound director), but we appreciate and love them not for their work in films or on television, but for their contribution to the development of musical culture.

In addition to studio engineers, who report to music producers, there are other engineering specialties in the music industry. Just list them (popular hardware and software brands are listed in parentheses).

Manufacturers of sound recording equipment set themselves the goal of transferring sound from the external environment to the medium. All new models are produced for the needs of studio engineers (AKG, Neumann, Sennheiser, Zoom, Focusrite).

Manufacturers of musical instruments create universal devices that can be used by both performers and producers. Any sound source can exist in any tradition, there is no framework here (Yamaha, Gibson, Fender, Akai, Korg, Clavia).

Specialists in room acoustics successfully solve the problems of designing both concert halls and recording rooms. For them, this is more a matter of physics and customer requirements, rather than art (although not for everyone).

Manufacturers of concert acoustic equipment set themselves both tasks: to convey live performance as efficiently as possible and to provide excellent sound of recorded phonograms in the studio - even in concert halls, even in DJ clubs or stadiums.

Studying the evolution of musical engineering culture, you can find many reasons for optimism. Perhaps it is the engineers who will make a decisive contribution to the unification of all musical traditions. I wouldn't be surprised if it's bioengineers who circumvent the current limitations of the human body.

Musical cultures instead of art

Consciously or not, we always try to identify the sources and primary sources (authors, owners) when we listen to music. This happens both at concerts and while listening to music recordings. It is normal to divide the information that comes to us into "someone else's" and "our own" - this mechanism refers to the basic human characteristics. So we draw a line between our own world, where we are free to do what we want, and the world of other people, where we should be more careful.
The "friend-friend" system allows you to represent music in seven states (see the seven sectors in the diagram). Let's try to use this theory to explain the weaknesses of modern music as an art compared to classical music. If these thoughts of mine seem too philosophical to you, you can read the paragraphs in bold and move on to the next section.
The seven states of music. Classification of music based on the theory of "friend - foe".

The music around us is a world of sounds that are not separated from other systems. We don't know how this music relates to other people and to us. We have not yet had time to understand whether it is worthy of our attention, but we no longer doubt that it exists.

Someone else's music is a protected area. We respect this combination of sounds, relate it to certain people, and do not interfere. Someone else's life follows its own rules, and this is its right.

Your own music is a familiar musical world in our head, which makes our life brighter, richer, more harmonious. These are the tunes we hum to ourselves, these are the images we play over and over again. We are sure that this music is our property, and do not think about how it is connected with other people and the world around us.

These three categories are worthy of separate study by philosophers, but at the moment we are much more interested in what happens at their intersection.

When we realize that some music around us is alien, connected with some phenomena and people, almost nothing changes for us. We still consider such music to be part of the world around us, that is, part of non-musical cultures. For example, when we visit a movie theater or an ice show, we hear background music and realize that the music here is just part of something non-musical (of course, if it's not a musical).

Recombination of musical values is the use of other people's ideas in your music world. When we weave a Bach or Cobain melody into our own lives, change that melody, add our own images, harmonies, or make a remix, that's exactly the kind of work we do - recombine and don't feel shy about it; but we do give credit to the authors.

Musical cultures are an attempt to find harmony between what sounds around us and the music in our soul. This is the joy of hearing a familiar tune on the bus; this is the moment when you started singing along to a drinking song or an artist at a concert. Musical cultures are our individual mechanism of adaptation to reality through music - it is an imprint of our soul in the world around us.

And now let's move on to the most important question - what is the art of music? How can the music of your soul simultaneously be alien and harmoniously complement the world around you?

A concert or performance is a situation in which music has every chance to become something more for the listener, because the degree of mutual influence of one's own and another's is determined in advance. We are told who is the author of the works, we respect them as strangers, but at the same time we have the opportunity to touch this culture, understand it, make it our own. This is how classical music becomes art.

Modern music has one big difference. Our "communication" with a device that plays audio recordings does not lend itself to the same unambiguous assessment as playing a musical instrument or attending a concert.

Yes, what we hear from the speakers or headphones comes from the outside world. At the same time, it is also something of our own, because we found the file on the network or on our device, we control the sound wave in time by pressing the "play" and "stop" buttons; and DJs can change the sound at all through mixing and special effects.

In order to consider a musical recording as a work of art, it must remain "alien" to us and others, we must have due respect for the authors, feel certain boundaries, but do we want this?

Remember how often you can't tell who the creator of a track is. Sometimes we know what radio is playing or what DJ is "playing", what is the name of the singer or singer, but almost 100% of the time we do not want to know who exactly is the music producer of the record. In contrast, by the way, to the classical tradition with its indispensable mention of composers; or cinema, where the role of the director is always and everywhere emphasized.

When we take an audio file and insert it into our playlist, we cut off the connection with the author, making his music a part of our daily life; and our respect for someone else's work is carried away somewhere on the periphery of consciousness. Many of us love the sound recordings of our playlist or DJ mix too much to "share" them with the creators. As a result, we ourselves become "authors-performers" who give music to the world by pressing the "play" button, mixing tracks. In this way, modern music becomes a musical culture, but loses the signs of musical art.

Music audio recordings become part of our daily life and culture, losing touch with their creators and the hallmarks of art. By sorting and playing tracks at a convenient time, listeners themselves become "authors-performers" of new works-playlists and DJ mixes.

The fact of the existence of many musical cultures (subcultures) will not be disputed by any art critic. Individual contemporary works, forms and genres have their own fans, but how do we relate to this diversity? How can we distinguish what we will call art after all?

In classical music, two mechanisms were developed that are still successfully used today.

The first criterion of a work of art is the presence of musical education among its creators - composers and performers. In the process of education, pupils and students are encouraged to respect the creativity of others and are taught to "master" it.

We have already talked enough about the problems of the academic tradition, and we will talk further, so we understand that this filter is still poorly applicable to modern music. Many, if not most, of the creators of the tracks we value are musical "non-learners" or "under-learners" simply because they didn't have to study to be performers, composers, conductors, or sound engineers. They acted on a whim, gathering talented people around them, creating their own standards of music production. Modern music is a combination of these accidents, the legacy of the geniuses of music production.

The second criterion that can be used in the classical tradition is expert opinions. The competence of musicologists includes reviews, scientific and journalistic publications, research, participation in the jury of ratings and awards. The results of this activity form the agenda for patrons and sponsors, state and non-profit organizations engaged in the preservation of cultural heritage.

Why is there no such expert structure around contemporary music?

Since 1958, musicians have received the Grammy Award, established by the Recording Industry Association of America. Among the nominations there are not only "Album of the Year" or "Record (single) of the Year", but also video clips, soundtracks, arrangement, and even a prize for the best cover. Notably, the award is given for " artistic achievement, technical excellence, and significant contribution to the development of sound recording without taking into account sales and chart positions." In some of the main categories, the award is given to both authors and producers with engineers.

In addition, not only in the United States, but also around the world in the second half of the XX century, music journalism and criticism developed quite actively. The sharks of the pen tried to analyze the upcoming albums, interviewed their creators, and created charts. The development of radio broadcasting in the FM band and the emergence of music TV channels were a major help.

Thanks to the achievements of the recording and broadcasting industries, by the ' 90s we had almost no doubt about the independent important role of many producers like Phil Spector or Brian Eno, because we saw an independent assessment of their work. At that time, it seemed that there was very little time left to wait until the approval of modern music as an independent part of the art sphere, music journalism and criticism could fulfill this mission, but the digital revolution came inopportunely. Magazines were closed one by one, and radio stations began to focus exclusively on ratings, leaving less and less space for constructive criticism and author programs.

It was extremely difficult for individual enthusiasts to fight these trends, because thanks to the availability of music creation technologies, the volume of audio recordings produced has grown significantly over two decades. Now dozens of critics, with all their desire, can not listen to millions of tracks and analyze them qualitatively. The Grammy Awards, of course, still have some weight, but it is hardly enough for a multipolar world.

The formation of a stable system of independent and unbiased evaluation by professional listeners (producers, critics, musicologists) can help to recognize contemporary music as one of the art directions.

Fortunately, for 2018, the situation no longer looks so hopeless. Special service technologies are being developed to adequately analyze huge volumes of music. For example, the algorithms of the Yandex.Music project help its editors to conduct research on the musical influence of Kazakh regions or the lexical development of Kazakh rap. The chatbot helps the Music Lovers of KZ community automate listening and voting in the framework of the Vkontakte Music service, during which hundreds of people compare up to four hundred songs and video clips a year, and as a result, ratings of unknown hits of KZ are published and awards are organized.

These examples may still look exotic and local, but they give hope for the early revival of independent professional evaluation in the music field of Kazakhstan. I would like to believe that an era will soon come when an" alien "objective view will translate the" own " music of individual musicians-producers from the sphere of private entertainment and fan culture to the public plane.

The influence of music on plants and the human body

To be honest, we have heard more than once about the influence of music on a living organism, on mood, on illness, and so on. Also, experiments were conducted more than once.

The first experiment. The same seeds were taken, planted in the same conditions, and everything that could affect their growth was observed.

The first group of seeds was placed under a 3-hour exposure to heavy rock

The second group of seeds under the " pop " music at the same time.

The third group is set to classical music, Bach music for 3 hours.

After a certain time, when the seeds have risen and grown, it turned out that the smallest plants are in the first group, then in the second and the tallest and healthiest in the third group.

Similar experiments were conducted more than once, and on other plants and evaluated the fruits of plants. The negative influence of heavy and popular music was noticed everywhere.

Here are recommendations for using music to treat certain diseases:

Mozart's works are suitable for stabilizing and activating brain activity. These can be the first and third movements from piano sonatas and concertos, "Rondo" from "Little Night Serenade".

Oginsky's polonaise, Liszt's Hungarian Rhapsody, and Beethoven's Fidelio will save you from a headache.

Insomnia can be cured with the suite" Peer Gynt " by Grieg," Sad Waltz " by Sibelius, and pieces by Tchaikovsky.

For hypertensive patients, it is useful to listen to Chopin's "Nocturne in D Minor", Mendelssohn's "Wedding March" and Bach's "Concerto in D Minor" for violin

Pleasant music has a beneficial effect on the blood. Your favorite tune causes an increase in blood lymphocytes, the body becomes easier to fight diseases.

Listening to the classics helps you easily memorize information.

Conclusion

Today, music is not only a way of self-affirmation or relaxation, but also a whole segment of the market where money is made. Recording studios produce music CDs with albums by contemporary artists and sell them in huge circulations. You can download mp3 for free on the Internet on specialized resources, so now you do not have to wait for a music program on TV or on the radio as before ‚Äì music has become more accessible.

Music is very rich in different directions and genres. Each of us can make a choice in favor of the musical genre that he likes best. Today, there are so many musical directions that it is not possible to tell about everything.
