GHB analogs are treated as controlled substances under Federal law only if intended for human consumption. According to 21 U.S.C. ¬ß 813, "a controlled substance analog(ue) shall, to the extent intended for human consumption, be treated, for the purposes of any Federal law as a controlled substance in Schedule I." Thus, authorities can prosecute drug offenses involving GHB analogs in the same manner as offenses involving GHB. (See 21 U.S.C. ¬ß 802(32) for the definition of a controlled substance analog(ue).)
